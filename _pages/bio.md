I am currently an Assistant Professor in the [School of Applied and Engineering Physics](https://www.aep.cornell.edu/) at Cornell University. From 2022–2025, I was a postdoc at Harvard, funded by a Junior Fellowship from the [Harvard Society of Fellows](https://socfell.fas.harvard.edu/). There, I worked on various problems in quantum and nonlinear optics.

I completed my PhD in the Physics Department at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (2016–2022), as a [Computational Science Graduate Fellow](https://www.krellinst.org/) of the U.S. Department of Energy (2016–2020) and a Dean’s Fellow of the MIT School of Science (2020–2022). For my thesis work, I was awarded the [Andrew M. Lockett III Memorial Prize](https://physics.mit.edu/). My primary thesis supervisor was [Prof. Marin Soljačić](https://www.rle.mit.edu/), and I also worked closely with [Prof. John Joannopoulos](https://physics.mit.edu/) (MIT) and [Prof. Ido Kaminer](https://kaminer.technion.ac.il/) (Technion), along with many collaborators.

In 2016, I received my Bachelor’s degree in Physics from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (2012–2016). For my bachelor’s thesis, I received the [LeRoy Apker Award](https://www.aps.org/) of the American Physical Society (citation: “for important advances in the field of photonics and exceptional leadership of the Society of Physics Students”).

Prior to that, I attended Stuyvesant High School in New York City, where I was born and raised.
